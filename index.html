ℹ️ About Us

TTC MUHANGA is committed to training qualified educators for the future.

General Information

TTC Muhanga, officially established on February 2, 2010, is a government-supported teacher training college managed in partnership with Rwanda’s Ministry of Education and the Anglican Diocese of Shyogwe. Situated in Mubuga Cell, approximately 4 km off the main highway and about 8 km from Muhanga District headquarters, it serves as a pivotal institution in the Southern Province. The college currently enrols around 625 students—roughly 400 female and 225 male—across four specialized departments: Teaching Modern Languages, Science & Mathematics, Social Sciences, and Early Childhood Education.

TTC Muhanga is guided by the motto “Knowledge, Success and Unity,” and employs a learner-centred teaching methodology training all tutors in this progressive approach. Its campus includes 15 classrooms, a computer lab, a modest library, teacher resource rooms, dormitories, a dining hall, and kitchen facilities. Practical learning is supported by vegetable and fruit gardens and workshops for creating teaching aids from local materials.

The college offers a broad curriculum that includes core subjects; Sciences (Mathematics, Physics, Biology, Chemistry), languages (English, French, Swahili & Kinyarwanda), Psychology, Entrepreneurship, Fine Arts, History, Geography, and Music. Over time, TTC Muhanga has undergone infrastructure expansion, including new facilities commissioned by the State Minister in July 2010. Today, it stands as a dynamic teacher training institution committed to producing skilled educators equipped to meet Rwanda’s evolving educational landscape.

Currently, the College is led by Principal Mukabatesi Jeanne D'Arc

Founded with a vision to nurture quality teachers for the nation.
